My work

I created this photo illustration for an a&e Sunday cover of The Oregonian. I started with a photograph of the Made in Portland sign. I changed the words on the sign and photographed a ribbon that became the sash over the deer's eyes.

For this two-page spread, we stitched together dozens of photos taken from a helicopter to show Portland's changing skyline. The challenge was to get the wealth of information, including each building's location, function, height, number of units and development status, organized in a way that made the page inviting and approachable.

Creativity can be more effective than technical tricks as I showed with this story on the gender gap in the Oregon Book Awards. I used my scissors and a couple of pages from a book to create the illustration.

I created this illustration for the a&e Sunday cover because, in Portland, the only thing more predictable than the arrival of new television shows each fall is the arrival of the rain.

The lightness and movement of this page do a great job of capturing the feeling of the story.

This page delivers a lot of information and is easy to navigate. I assembled all the tools mentioned in the story and photographed them on my dining room table so that each description is aligned with the item discussed.

I art directed a freelancer to create the illustration for this page I designed about various types of cruises, from the newsest huge ocean liner to short overnight jaunts between Seattle and Vancouver, B.C.

I especially enjoy the challenge of working primarily with type. The directness of this package effectively makes the point of how serious a problem food waste is in this country.

For the cover of a&e's BBQ special issue, I wrote the section name, header and pig illustration with a squirt bottle full of BBQ sauce onto parchment paper and photographed it.

As a designer, I know that sometimes the answer is to get out of the way of a mouthwatering photo.

At work I am designing for a mass-market audience, but sometimes at home I'm just designing a flyer for my daughter's school.

About me

I am a communications professional with a passion for visual storytelling and a wealth of experience in design and editing. An adept problem solver, I am skilled at prioritizing resources and achieving demanding deadlines. I am happiest professionally when I am challenged and able to make a product or a situation better. I love to help other people and I thrive in team environments.
