My work

I created this photo illustration for an a&e Sunday cover of The Oregonian. I started with a photograph of the Made in Portland sign. I changed the words on the sign and photographed a ribbon that became the sash over the deer's eyes.

For this two-page spread, we stitched together dozens of photos taken from a helicopter to show Portland's changing skyline. This package which took two broadsheet pages shows the different areas along the waterfront and the development planned for each. The challenge was to get the wealth of information, including each building's location, function, height, number of units and development status, oraganized in way that made the page inviting and approachable.

For the cover of a&e's BBQ special issue, I wrote out the section name, header and pig illustration with a squirt bottle full of BBQ sauce onto parchment paper and then photographed it.

I created this illustration for the a&e Sunday cover because, in Portland, the only thing as predictable as the arrival of new television shows each fall is the arrival of the rain.

Creativity can trump technology. For a story on the gender gap in the Oregon Book Awards, I got out my scissors and couple pages from a book.

The lightness and movement of this page do a great job of capturing the feeling of the story.

This page delivers a lot of information and is easy to navigate. I assembled all the tools mentioned in the story on my dining room table so that each description is alligned with the item discussed.

Sometimes, a simple, boldly colored page is the way to catch someone's attention, like with this grill illustration I created for the Food section.

I really enjoy the challenge of working primarily with type. The directness of this package effectively makes the point of how serious a problem food waste is in this country.

As a designer, I know that sometimes the answer is to get out of the way of a mouth-watering photo.

Even though for work I am designing for a mass-market audience, sometimes at home I'm just designing a flyer for my daughter's school.

About me

I am a communications professional with a passion for visual storytelling and a wealth of experience in design and editing. An adept problem solver, I am skilled at prioritizing resources and achieving demanding deadlines. I am happiest professionally when I am challenged and able to make a product or a situation better. I love to help other people and I thrive in team environments.
