Work

I created this photo illustration for an a&e Sunday cover of The Oregonian. I started with a photograph of the Made in Portland sign. I changed the words on the sign and I took a photogrpah of a gold ribbon that happened to be handy, changed its color in Photoshop and added it to the illustration.

Dozens of photos taken from a helicopter were stitched together to create panoramas of Portland's changing skyline. This package which took two broadsheet pages shows the different areas along the waterfront and the development planned for each. The design challenge was to orangize all the disparate pieces of information and keep the page inviting and approachable.

For the cover of a&e's BBQ special issue, I wrote out the section name, header and pig illustration with a squirt bottle full of bbq sauce onto parchment paper and then photographed it.

Sometimes, a simple, boldly colored page is the way to catch someone's attention, like with this grill illustration I created for the Food section.

Technology isn't always the answer. For a story on the gender gap in winners of the Oregon Book Awards, I got out my scissors and couple pages from a book.

The lightness and movement of this page do a great job of capturing the feeling of the story.

This page has a lot of information to get across, but makes it easy to dive right in. I assembled all the tools mentioned in the story on my dining room table and photographed them so that each graph points right to the item discussed.

About me

I am a communications professional with a passion for visual storytelling and a wealth of experience in design and editing. An adept problem solver, I am skilled at prioritizing resources and achieving demanding deadlines. I am happiest professionally when I am challenged and able to make a product or a situation better. I love to help other people and thrive in team environments.
